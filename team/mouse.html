The beginning

I'm 22, born and raised in Köln, and I still live here. Same streets I've been tagging since I was 15. How would I describe myself? Loud, broke, loyal as hell. The friend who'll stay up all night helping you edit your shit even if I have nothing to eat the next day. A bit chaotic, always hyped, zero filter.

I started making art the second I could hold a pencil — that kid in class drawing on every desk and notebook. It got serious in high school when I met Katze and we started feeding off each other's ideas.

The art

Digital illustration with a street-style edge. I do graphic design work too. My style is chaotic by intent — bold, loud, the visual equivalent of how I talk. If something can be bigger, brighter, or more in-your-face, I'll usually push it that way.

What I do for HART

I'm the hype. I do graphic work for posts and announcements, I help artists who are nervous about uploading, I argue with people online when they say HART is just another NFT site (it isn't). Growth on a project like this isn't paid ads — it's showing up every day and actually caring. That part I'm good at.

Why this matters to me

Every artist friend I have has had a moment where AI took something away from them — a commission, recognition, the feeling that their work was worth anything. If I can help even one of them feel like there's somewhere their work counts again, I've done my job.
